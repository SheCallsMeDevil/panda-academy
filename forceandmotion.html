b) Can the average speed be ever zero? Explain.

Ans : No, average speed can never be zero as long as there is some distance traveled over a period of time.if you travel a certain distance, your total distance traveled is not zero. Thus your average speed won't be zero.

c) Explain the condition of the velocities of the two bodies in motion when:
i) their velocities are added for relative velocity.
ii) their velocities are subtracted for relative velocity.

Ans : The velocities of the two bodies are added for relative velocity if two bodies are moving in opposite direction with same or different velocities.
The velocities of the two bodies are subtracted for relative velocity if two bodies are moving in the same direction with same or different velocities.

d) What is indicated by the statement "acceleration of a body is 10 m/s2"?
Ans : If a body's acceleration is 10 m/s2, it means that the velocity of the body is changing by 10 meters per second every second.

For example, if an object starts from rest (velocity = 0 m/s) and has a constant acceleration of 10 m/s2, its velocity after 1 second would be 10 m/s, after 2 seconds it would be 20 m/s, after 3 seconds it would be 30 m/s, and so on.

e) Describe the conditions when the acceleration of a body is positive, negative and zero?

Ans : The acceleratioin of a body will be positive if the velocity of the body increases.
The acceleratioin of a body will be negative if the velocity of the body decreases.
The acceleratioin of a body will be zero if the velocity of the body remains same or constant.
